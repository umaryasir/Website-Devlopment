Engineering education is the activity of teaching knowledge and principles to the professional practice of engineering. It includes an initial education (bachelor's and/or master's degree), and any advanced education and specializations that follow. Engineering education is typically accompanied by additional postgraduate examinations and supervised training as the requirements for a professional engineering license. The length of education, and training to qualify as a basic professional engineer, is typically 8–12 years, with 15–20 years for an engineer who takes responsibility for major projects. Birla Institute of Technology and Science

Birla Institute of Technology & Science, Pilani (shortened BITS Pilani or BITS) is a private institute of higher education and a deemed university under Section 3 of the UGC Act 1956.[12] The institute is one among the 6 Indian 'Institutes of Eminence' recognized by the Government of India in 2018.[13][14] It is established across 4 campuses and has 15 academic departments. It focuses primarily on higher education in engineering and sciences and on its management programme.[15] The institute was established in its present form in 1964. During this period, the institute's transformation from a regional engineering college to a national university was backed by G.D. Birla. BITS, since then, has expanded to campuses at Pilani, Goa, Hyderabad and Dubai. BITS conducts the All-India computerized entrance examination, BITSAT (BITS Admission Test).[16][17] Admission is merit-based, as assessed by the BITSAT examination.[18][19] The fully residential institute is privately supported

Vellore Institute Of Technology

Vellore Institute of Technology (VIT) is a private deemed university and an Institute of Eminence located in the outskirts of Vellore, Tamil Nadu, India. It is one of the top engineering colleges of India. It was ranked NO. 1 by NIRF - ARIIA in 2019 among private engineering colleges. Founded in 1984, as Vellore Engineering College, by G. Viswanathan, the institution offers 20 undergraduate, 34 postgraduate, four integrated and four research programs. It is majorly known for it's placement, diversity and research and training opportunities. It has campuses in Vellore, Chennai, Bhopal and Amravati Andhra Pradesh.VIT admits students through its own engineering entrance exam,called the Vellore Institute of Technology Engineering Entrance Examination (VITEEE). Manipal Institute of Technology

The Manipal Institute of Technology (shortened MIT Manipal or MIT) is a constituent institution of Manipal Academy of Higher Education that specializes in the fields of engineering and technology. MIT contains 16 academic departments and awards undergraduate, graduate and post graduate degrees.[1] Established in 1957 in Manipal, Karnataka, India, it is one of the first self-financed colleges in India.[3] It has been consistently ranked as one of the top colleges in India for engineering education. Until the early nineties, the institution was affiliated to Karnatak University, University of Mysore, and then to Mangalore University. After the establishment of Manipal Academy of Higher Education/Manipal University in 1993, MIT became a constituent institution of Manipal Academy of Higher Education. the institute boasts a rich student culture and is constantly revisited by alumni.
